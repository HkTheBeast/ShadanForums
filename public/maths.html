Computer Oriented Statistical Methods

About The Subject :


Overview :


Computer Oriented Statistical Methods (COSM) focuses on applying statistical techniques and methods using computers. It combines the principles of statistics with computational tools to analyze, interpret, and visualize data efficiently. This subject helps in making data-driven decisions using programming and software-based analysis.

Key Concepts of COSM:

  • Data Collection and Classification: Gathering raw data and organizing it into meaningful categories for analysis.
  • Measures of Central Tendency: Statistical measures like mean, median, and mode used to find the central value of a dataset.
  • Measures of Dispersion: Techniques such as range, variance, and standard deviation that show how data is spread or varied.
  • Correlation and Regression: Methods to identify relationships between variables and predict outcomes based on existing data.
  • Probability and Distributions: Study of random events and probability models like binomial, Poisson, and normal distribution.
  • Sampling and Estimation: Techniques for selecting representative data samples and estimating population parameters.
  • Hypothesis Testing: Statistical method to test assumptions and make inferences based on sample data.
  • Use of Computers in Statistics: Application of programming languages like Python, R, or MATLAB to perform statistical calculations, simulations, and graphical representation of data.

Applications of COSM:
COSM is widely used in business analytics, scientific research, economics, healthcare, and data science. It helps in identifying patterns, forecasting trends, and supporting evidence-based decision making.

Conclusion:
Computer Oriented Statistical Methods bridges the gap between statistics and computing. It enables students to apply statistical theories practically using computational tools, making it a vital subject for modern data analysis and research.
